Wrap-Genius

A personal journey into better food labeling.

To Next Section

The food we buy matters.

As our food systems become increasingly
complex, the food we choose to consume affects
not only our own health, but also the environment,
our ethical framework, the Earth's biodiversity,
and so much more.

But much of the data of our food purchases remains
hidden to us as end consumers. Food labels only give us
a basic snapshot. And pushes for better labeling are met
by slow-moving governments and companies resistant
to change.

This isn’t good enough.

We, as consumers, deserve
to know more about our food.

So, I went on a mission to come up with
something better. For 10 weeks, I carefully
tracked all of my grocery purchases in hopes of
creating a better and more visual look into my
food purchases.

A label 2.0 sorta speak.

Here’s what I found.

The Future

By carefully tracking my own grocery purchase data, I was able to gain a better and more visual understanding into how I consume groceries: where they come from, which ones have GMOs, which ones are the best and worst for me, and so much more.

I believe the food industry has much to gain from giving consumers access to this data, in terms of helping people make more informed choices and ultimately increase trust in the places where they shop.
